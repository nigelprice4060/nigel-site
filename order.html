Force Feedback Steering Wheel [Individual Project]

This is a custom Force Feedback Steering Wheel that uses a repurposed hoverboard motor for torque. A force feedback steering wheel allows me to input mechanical turning data into the computer with realistic feedback, as the (hoverboard) motor can use dynamic torque to change the force to turn the steering wheel which can physically simulate elements such as driving on different road surfaces, turning at different speeds, and more. With that, I've used FFBeast software to analyze the inputs and connect it to a driving/racing game of my choice. It features 12 3D printed PLA plastic parts designed in Solidworks, a USB button board, a Servo Motor Controller, a Rotary Encoder, and a 24V power supply. Through this project, I've learned about compatible electronics, wiring electronics, alot about mechanical fastening, how to solder effectively, how to better CAD in Solidworks, and root cause analysis. Duck Childrens Toy [Group Project]

Me and a group of 3 others were tasked with designing and building a childrens toy for our final project that followed appropriate universal design principles. Our idea was a waterproof Duck Toy that could move in water by spinning its legs with differential gearing and a motor inside. My task was to help design, Model, print, and assemble the outer body. I had to maximize space for the internal components which explains the large body. In order to have the head move, I used a ball and socket joint. This admittedly took a couple of tries to perfect as it was mostly a guess and check situation with different measurements each time. The body was sealed with a clear silicone adheasive.
